Why?

Over 30 Million people have taken a genetic test so far. Genetic tests reveal mutations whithin a person, and while these can be used for basic profiling, such as gender, skin color, and other features, these genes can also be used to highlight mutations which could potentialy cause diseases such as cancer, sickle cell, adhd, and more.

How it Works.

Databases.

Hundreds of Databases exist with Phenotype-Genotype associations for various diseases. Using a novel fuzzy search algorithm, is able to levrage all of them to search for variations in your DNA that could signal certain diseases.

Models.

DYGD also includes alot of ML models created by researchers and the team that can signal that you have additional diseases, that may not be studied enough to have comprehensive G-P associations listed, altogether giving you more information about yourself. These models aren't avaliable anywhere else.

Summary.

At the end of all checks, a summary is returned to you, with all detected anomalies.

Get Started.

Step 1. Get The Right Files

Make sure you have FASTA files, in the .fa Format. These are the most common genetic file format, so make sure to check twice! You will need 22 of them, for all 22 Chromosomes

Step 2. Name your Files Correctly

Our service has no way of knowing which file is which, as all of them are roughly similar! Make sure you get all of them named "CHR-1" for chromosome 1 (without the quotations), "CHR-2" for chromosome two, and so on and so forth. We would advise you make a new folder and copy them to this folder.

Step 3. Browse for your Files

Click the "Browse..." Button, and navigate to the folder where your scans are, and select all of them.

Step 4. Wait...

Click Upload, and wait, Depending on internet speeds the test could take anywhere from 2 - 10 minutes.
